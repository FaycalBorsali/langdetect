Quintus Caecilius Metellus Balearicus (b. c. 170 BC) was a son of Quintus Caecilius Metellus Macedonicus. He was a Consul in 123 BC and a Censor in 120 BC, dominated Sardinia and conquered the Balearic Islands - for what he earned his cognomen and the honours of Triumph - establishing at Palma de Mallorca a colony of 3,000 Romans and Iberians.He was the father of:Quintus Caecilius Metellus NeposCaecilia Metella/Caecilia MetellaCaecilia Metella, wife of Appius Claudius PulcherFurther readingManuel Dejante Pinto de Magalhães Arnao Metello and João Carlos Metello de Nápoles, "Metellos de Portugal, Brasil e Roma", Torres Novas, 1998   Category:Roman Republican consuls Category:Roman censors Category:2nd century BC Romans Category:History of the Balearic Islands Metellus Balearicus, Quintus Category:170 BC births Category:Year of death unknownca:Quint Cecili Metel Baleàric de:Quintus Caecilius Metellus Balearicus es:Quinto Cecilio Metelo Baleárico fr:Quintus Caecilius Metellus Baliaricus it:Quinto Cecilio Metello Balearico hu:Quintus Caecilius Metellus Balearicus nl:Quintus Caecilius Metellus Balearicus pl:Kwintus Cecyliusz Metellus Balearyjski
